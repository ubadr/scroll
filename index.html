The Origins

Long ago, a wise philosopher observed the world around him and noticed that everything in the universe seemed to have a complementary opposite.

He called these opposites Yin and Yang. He believed that they were interconnected and interdependent, each containing a small piece of the other.

࿊

A Powerful Representation

To symbolize the idea, he created the iconic Yin Yang symbol, with its black and white halves and smaller circles of the opposite color.

They represent the balance and harmony that can be achieved when these seemingly opposing forces work together.

࿊

Finding Balance

As the philosopher continued to study the world, he saw that everything had its own Yin and Yang energy, including human beings.

He believed that the key to a happy and fulfilled life was to find balance between these energies within oneself and with the world around them.

"Yin and yang are two halves that together complete wholeness. They represent the interdependent nature of all things in the universe."
